From April 24th to April 26th, I had the pleasure to attend the 27th European Symposium on Artificial Neural Networks (ESANN) at Bruges, Belgium for the second time. Again, there were a lot of interesting research sessions and I had the opportunity to discuss our papers Predicting vehicle behaviour using LSTMs and a vector power representation for spatial positions  and Short-term trajectory planning using reinforcement learning within a neuromorphic control architecture  during the poster sessions of the conference. If you are interested in this research, you can find the pdf files of both papers as well as the posters online linked in the publication list in my resume.
