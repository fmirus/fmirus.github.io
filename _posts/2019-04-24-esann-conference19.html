From April 24th to April 26th, I had the pleasure to attend the 27th European Symposium on Artificial Neural Networks (ESANN) at Bruges, Belgium for the second time. Again, there were a lot of interesting research sessions and I had the opportunity to discuss our papers Predicting vehicle behaviour using LSTMs and a vector power representation for spatial positions  and Short-term trajectory planning using reinforcement learning within a neuromorphic control architecture  during the poster sessions of the conference. You can also find the pdf files of the posters online linked in the publication list in my resume.
