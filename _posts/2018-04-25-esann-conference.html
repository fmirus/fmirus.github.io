From April 25th to April 27th, I had the pleasure to attend the 26th European Symposium on Artificial Neural Networks (ESANN) at Bruges, Belgium. I had the opportunity to attend several interesting research sessions and to discuss our paper Towards cognitive automotive environment modelling: reasoning based on vector representations  during the poster session of the conference. If you are interested in this research, you can find the paper and the poster online. Furthermore, I got a glimpse at the beautiful midieval city of Bruges during the social events of the conference, which included a guided tour of Bruges and a visit of a famous brewery.
