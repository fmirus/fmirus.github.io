After five and a half exciting years, today marks my last day at BMW Group. From beginning as a Ph.D. candidate in 2015 researching neuromorphic computing, distributed representations and spiking neural networks in the car to working as a Research Scientist on neuromorphic engineering, power-efficient computing for AI, and robotics, it always was a great pleasure and utmost honor to work for this amazing company.

During my time at BMW Group, I received the Ph.D. degree for my thesis Towards a cognitive automotive environment model: a novel approach based on distributed representations and spiking neural networks from Technical University of Munich, I co-organized the workshop Towards Cognitive Vehicles: perception, learning and decision making under real-world constraints. Is bio-inspiration helpful? at IROS 2019 and led (parts of) several projects on neuromorphic computing and AI accelerators.

Apart from the opportunity to work on such exciting research topics and my personal and professional growth, I was fortunate enough to meet a lot of truly amazing colleagues. In particular, I would like to thank Hans-Joerg Voegel, my manager and mentor of my Ph.D. work, Martin Arend and Michael Wuertenberger, my department leaders during my Ph.D. years, as well as some great colleagues: Mohsen Kaboli, Oliver Wick, Viktor Rack, Christoph Segler and many more. It's been a pleasure working with you. Thank you.

While I am sad to close the door on this chapter of my career, I couldn’t be more excited for the next one. As of May 1st, I will be joining Intel Labs as Researcher Dependable Automated Driving and Robotics. I'm curious and excited about the challenges and opportunities this next step holds for me.
